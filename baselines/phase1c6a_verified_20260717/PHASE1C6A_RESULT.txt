Verified guarantee:

The fixed e0 -> e1 -> e2 construction is replaced by a reusable fresh
successor rule that may be applied repeatedly to construct finite traces
of arbitrary epoch-chain length.

Every accepted epoch requires matching prior issuance.

Current acceptance carries matching current-epoch evidence.

Stale acceptance and every backward traversal of a recorded epoch edge
require prior compromise of the corresponding generalized verifier.

Conflicting direct-edge acceptance histories require prior verifier
compromise.

Generalization method:

The model proves local transition invariants over a generic
lower_epoch -> higher_epoch edge. Repeated application covers longer
finite epoch chains without enumerating fixed epoch names.

Explicit scope:

- reusable fresh epoch-successor transition
- finite traces of unbounded selected length
- generic current-epoch acceptance
- persistent verifier compromise
- repeated one-edge rollback after compromise
- stale acceptance after one or more rollback steps
- local transition safety invariants

Excluded from this phase:

- arithmetic epoch numbering
- induction inside Tamarin over natural numbers
- C5C revocation/transparency composition
- unrelated-contract global ordering
- implementation-level key storage
