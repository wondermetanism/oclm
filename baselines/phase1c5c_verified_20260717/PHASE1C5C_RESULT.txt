Verified guarantee:

Every final composed acceptance is anchored in the previously verified
C4B lineage, C5A revocation and C5B transparency contracts.

A clean acceptance carries current-lineage, active-certificate and
canonical-transparency evidence at the acceptance event.

Acceptance under a revoked state requires either a prior missing
revocation update or prior revocation-verifier compromise.

Forked transparency acceptance and conflicting composed roots require
prior Transparency Log compromise.

Stale-lineage acceptance requires prior lineage-verifier compromise.

Composition method:

The component internals are intentionally opaque. Phase 1-C5C uses
assume-guarantee contract handles and does not duplicate the internal
rules of C4B, C5A or C5B.

Explicit scope:

- finite lineage state: current or stale-after-compromise
- finite certificate state: active or revoked
- finite transparency checkpoint: s1 canonical or forked root
- clean composed acceptance
- missing-revocation-update exception
- revocation-verifier-compromise exception
- lineage-verifier-compromise exception
- Transparency Log compromise and conflicting roots

Excluded from this phase:

- component-internal cryptographic rules
- Merkle-tree internals
- gossip availability
- witness quorum
- unbounded epochs
- unbounded revocation states
- unbounded transparency checkpoints
